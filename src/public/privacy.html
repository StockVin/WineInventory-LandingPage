1. Introduction

At WineInventory (hereinafter, "we", "our" or "the Company") we respect your privacy...

This policy explains what information we collect...

2. Data Controller

Controller: WineInventory
Contact email: privacy@wineinventory.com

3. Data We Collect

We may collect and process the following personal data:

  • Contact data: first name, last name, email address, phone number.
  • Account information: username, encrypted password and account preferences.
  • Business data: information about wineries, batches, stock, orders and transactions related to wine management.
  • Payment data: information necessary to process payments (where applicable)...
  • Technical metadata: IP address, browser type, device information and activity logs.
  • Cookies and similar technologies: see Cookies section.

4. Purposes and Legal Bases for Processing

We process your data for the following purposes...

  • Account management and service delivery: performance of the contract between you and us.
  • Customer support and communication: legitimate interest and, where applicable, your consent.
  • Billing and legal compliance: compliance with legal and tax obligations.
  • Marketing and commercial communications: your explicit consent...
  • Security and fraud prevention: legitimate interest to protect our systems and users.
  • Analytics and service improvement: legitimate interest and/or your consent for analytical cookies.

5. Cookies and Similar Technologies

We use cookies to improve the user experience, analyze site usage and provide functionality. Cookies may be:

  • Essential: necessary for site operation (e.g., authentication).
  • Performance/analytics: to measure and improve site usage (e.g., Google Analytics).
  • Functional: remember preferences and customizations.
  • Advertising: to deliver relevant ads when applicable.

You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings. Please note that disabling essential cookies may affect the functioning of the service.

6. Third Parties and Service Providers

We may share data with providers who perform services on our behalf, including:

  • Payment platforms: gateways to process charges (e.g., Stripe, PayPal).
  • Hosting and storage providers: to host data and backups.
  • Analytics tools: such as Google Analytics.
  • Email services: to send notifications and communications (e.g., campaigns and confirmations).

All providers with access to data are bound by contracts that require them to protect the information and use it only for contracted purposes.

7. International Transfers

Your data may be stored and processed on servers located outside your country. When international transfers occur, we will adopt appropriate safeguards (standard contractual clauses, adequacy decisions or other applicable guarantees) to protect your data.

8. Data Retention

We will retain your personal data for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes described in this policy, to comply with legal obligations and to resolve liabilities. Criteria for determining retention periods include the nature of the data, the purpose of processing and applicable legal obligations.

9. Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your data against loss, misuse, unauthorized access, disclosure and alteration. However, no system is 100% secure; we recommend taking precautions with your account (e.g., using strong, unique passwords).

10. Minors

Our service is not directed to minors. We do not knowingly collect data from minors. If you believe we have collected information about a minor without appropriate consent, contact us so we can remove that information.

11. Rights of Data Subjects

You may exercise the following rights, where applicable under the relevant law:

  • Access: request a copy of the data we process about you.
  • Rectification: correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure ("right to be forgotten"): request deletion of your data when there is no legitimate reason to retain it.
  • Restriction of processing: request that we restrict the use of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Objection: object to processing based on your particular situation.
  • Portability: receive your data in a structured format and transfer it to another controller, where applicable.
  • Withdraw consent: where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.

To exercise your rights, contact us at privacy@wineinventory.com. We will respond in accordance with applicable law and within the legally established timeframes.

12. Complaints

If you believe your rights have not been properly addressed, you may file a complaint with the competent data protection authority in your country.

13. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy periodically. We will publish the updated version on our site with the date of the last update. We recommend that you review this page regularly.
